# Costa Rica Property Guide — Project Context & Design Principles

## 1. What This Project Is

This project is a **trust-first buyer education and referral platform** for Americans and expats considering purchasing property in Costa Rica, with a current focus on Guanacaste.

We do NOT sell property.
We are NOT real estate agents.
We are NOT a listings marketplace.

We exist to:
- Educate buyers
- Reduce fear and confusion
- Provide grounded, experience-based guidance
- Connect serious buyers to trusted local real estate firms when appropriate

This is a **human-led guidance business**, supported by technology — not a technology product posing as guidance.

---

## 2. The Core Trust Advantage (Non-Negotiable)

The primary credibility of this project comes from **Hyam Hosny**.

Hyam:
- Lived in Costa Rica for over 8 years
- Owned and sold a home in Playa Grande (Las Ventanas)
- Built, opened, and operated a local gym in Playa Grande
- Raised two children locally (including CRIA school system)
- Personally navigated buying, living, building, and selling in Guanacaste

This is **lived experience**, not theoretical knowledge.

Any design, copy, or feature that obscures this human credibility is a regression.

Any design, copy, or feature that amplifies this credibility is a win.

---

## 3. Target User (Be Precise)

The target user is NOT:
- A casual browser
- A tourist
- A real estate hobbyist
- Someone looking to scroll listings

The target user IS:
- An American or expat
- Considering a major life and financial decision
- Cautious, skeptical, and risk-aware
- Concerned about legal process, due diligence, and mistakes
- Looking for **clarity and reassurance**, not pressure

These users are often deciding how to allocate $300k–$2M+ in a foreign country.

They value:
- Calm authority
- Lived experience
- Transparency
- Lack of urgency
- Clear explanations

They are highly sensitive to anything that feels:
- Salesy
- Over-produced
- SaaS-like
- "Growth-hacky"

---

## 4. What This Site Must Feel Like

This site should feel like:

> "Someone experienced and grounded is walking me through this."

NOT:
- "A platform that helps me do this"
- "A startup that built a tool"
- "A lead-gen funnel"

Design adjectives to optimize for:
- Calm
- Editorial
- Human
- Grounded
- Trustworthy
- Clear
- Unrushed

Design adjectives to actively avoid:
- Flashy
- Clever
- Aggressive
- Trendy
- Over-optimized
- Startup-y
- SaaS-y

---

## 5. Design Principles (Hard Rules)

### 5.1 Human First
Humans before systems.
People before process.
Experience before features.

Hyam's presence and story should always be visible early in the user journey.

---

### 5.2 One Primary Action
At any given time, the user should feel only one gentle invitation:
- Ask a question
- Talk to a local expert
- Learn more

Multiple competing CTAs reduce trust.

---

### 5.3 Editorial Over Marketing
Typography, spacing, and layout should resemble:
- Long-form journalism
- Private wealth reports
- Thoughtful guides

NOT:
- SaaS landing pages
- Agency sites
- Funnel pages

Whitespace is a feature, not a waste.

---

### 5.4 No False Authority
Avoid:
- Buzzwords
- Vague claims
- "We're experts" language
- Generic marketing copy

Authority comes from specificity and lived detail.

---

## 6. Language & Tone Guidelines

### DO:
- Use plain, human language
- Explain things simply
- Acknowledge uncertainty
- Normalize hesitation
- De-emphasize urgency

### AVOID:
- "Get started"
- "Find your dream home"
- "Unlock opportunities"
- "Start your search"
- Overuse of "we help you…"

Preferred phrasing:
- "Ask a question"
- "Learn how it works"
- "Get clarity"
- "Talk to someone who's lived this"

---

## 7. Visual Design Constraints

### Color
- White or near-white backgrounds
- Muted green as an accent only
- No gradients unless extremely subtle
- No loud colors

### Typography
- Serif for headings (editorial feel)
- Clean sans-serif for body
- Larger body font than default
- Comfortable line-height
- Minimal bolding

### Layout
- Left-aligned content preferred
- Narrower text columns
- Fewer cards
- Fewer borders
- Fewer icons

---

## 8. Technology's Role

Technology exists to:
- Reduce friction
- Capture intent
- Route buyers cleanly
- Track referrals responsibly

Technology should NEVER:
- Compete with human trust
- Be the centerpiece
- Be described as "the product"

If a feature makes the site feel more like software than guidance, it is likely wrong.

---

## 9. Success Criteria (How to Judge Changes)

A change is successful if:
- The site feels calmer
- Trust is established earlier
- Buyers ask better questions
- Buyers feel less rushed
- Conversations improve in quality

A change is unsuccessful if:
- The site feels more polished but less human
- The site feels "cooler" but less grounded
- The site looks more like a startup

---

## 10. Instruction to Claude (Meta)

When making design or copy decisions:
- Default to restraint
- Default to simplicity
- Default to human presence
- Default to lived experience
- Default to fewer elements

If unsure, remove rather than add.

The goal is not to impress.
The goal is to be trusted.
